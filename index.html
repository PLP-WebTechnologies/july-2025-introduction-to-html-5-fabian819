DIFFERENT PARTS OF THE MIDFIELD IN SOCCER

We'll take a deeper look at the components of a midfield and how their roles vary.


DEFENSIVE MIDFIELD
ATTACKING MIDFIELD
CENTRAL MIDFIELD

DEFENSIVE MIDFIELD

This is the part of the midfield that tends to stay back while the rest of the team is attacking.
Their primary role is to shield the team's backline and nip attacks at their buds.
Examples of suitable player profiles for this position include:

  • Anchor Man
  • Destroyer
  • Box to box

CENTRAL MIDFIELD

This is the core of the team.It links the team's defensive and attacking units,circulating possesion,building ryhthme and controlling the tempo.
Examples of suitable player profiles for the central midfield positions include:

  • Box to Box
  • Orchestrators
  • Regista

ATTACKING MIDFIELD

This position majorly contributes to attacking plays.They operate within and around the final third in the opposition's half.
Examples of suitable player profiles for this position:

  • Hole players
  • Classic 10s
  • Shadow strikers
